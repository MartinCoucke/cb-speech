It’s all about the role of money - speech by Nathanaël Benjamin (2026-07-21)
Nathanaël Benjamin, Executive Director for Financial Stability Strategy and Risk at the Bank of England, presents a conceptual framework for understanding central bank policy through the three core functions of money: store of value, unit of account, and medium of exchange. He applies this framework to current financial stability topics including bank capital requirements, non-bank finance regulation, and hedge fund leverage. The speech is primarily focused on financial stability policy rather than interest rate or monetary policy decisions.
This speech is about financial stability frameworks and macroprudential policy, not monetary policy or interest rate decisions. There is no discussion of the rate-setting cycle, inflation outlook, or MPC deliberations.
  • “Our role is to ensure trust in money.”
  • “The relevant policy question is not whether capital is good or bad for growth in a binary sense, but rather what level and structure of capital requirements most effectively support sustainable growth by appropriately balancing resilience and efficiency.”
