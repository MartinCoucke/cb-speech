Barr, Deregulating in a Financial Boom: What Could Go Wrong? (2026-06-06)
Governor Michael Barr delivered a speech at American University warning against the current wave of bank deregulation, arguing that reductions in capital requirements, looser supervision, and weakening liquidity rules increase financial stability risks. He drew on historical crises and academic research to contend that short-term benefits of deregulation are outweighed by long-term costs. The speech is focused entirely on prudential regulation and financial stability, not on interest rates or the monetary policy stance.
This speech addresses bank capital, supervision, and financial stability regulation rather than the interest rate or balance sheet decisions of the FOMC. It is not a monetary policy speech and warrants a score of 0 with low confidence.
