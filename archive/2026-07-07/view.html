Waller, Two Thoughts on the Transmission of Monetary Policy (2026-07-06)
Governor Waller delivers a conceptual speech at a Bank of Italy conference discussing two aspects of monetary policy transmission: the importance of initial conditions in assessing policy effects, and the benefits and pitfalls of forward guidance. He draws on lessons from the 2021-2023 inflation episode to illustrate how nonlinear dynamics and rigid forward guidance can complicate policymaking. The speech is largely academic and retrospective, without explicit signals about the near-term policy path.
The speech offers no clear directional signal on current rate policy—it is analytical and historical in nature. Waller neither signals imminent easing nor tightening, making a neutral score appropriate, though the emphasis on flexibility and the critique of overly rigid forward guidance could be read as preserving optionality.
  • “Forward guidance can impair policy transmission if the guidance is too strong or rigid.”
  • “In these divergent situations, it's much harder to give forward guidance, and, as a result, it is less useful.”
