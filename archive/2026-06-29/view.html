Speech: Additional Monetary Policy Tools: Reflections and a New Framework (2026-06-28)
RBA Assistant Governor Christopher Kent outlines a new Framework for Additional Monetary Policy Tools at low interest rates, drawing lessons from the pandemic-era use of the Term Funding Facility, bond purchases, forward guidance, and yield targets. The speech emphasises that the cash rate remains the primary instrument and that additional tools carry significant costs and risks that must be carefully managed. The framework is forward-looking and preparatory rather than signalling any imminent policy change.
The speech is neutral in tone—it is a policy-framework document rather than a signal of current easing or tightening. While it discusses readiness for low-rate environments and acknowledges a potential earlier/more decisive response to disinflationary shocks, it explicitly reaffirms the cash rate as the primary tool and stresses the costs and limits of unconventional measures.
  • “The cash rate target remains our primary and preferred instrument for achieving low inflation and full employment.”
  • “The most important support during the pandemic came from lowering the cash rate to historically low levels and keeping it there. Additional tools can reinforce that support, but their effects – beyond addressing severe market strains – are likely to be more marginal and their risks need to be managed carefully.”
