Michele Bullock: Opening Statement to the Senate Economics Legislation Committee (Budget Estimates 2026&#8211;2027) (2026-06-09)
RBA Governor Michele Bullock addressed the Senate Economics Legislation Committee, outlining the Bank's decision to raise the cash rate by 75 basis points in 2026 in response to a resurgence in inflation driven by strong domestic demand and a Middle East conflict boosting energy prices. She indicated inflation remains too high, with headline CPI expected to peak above 4.5% in the June quarter and underlying inflation staying above target until mid-2027. The Board signalled it will do whatever is necessary to restore price stability, even as growth and employment are expected to soften.
The speech is clearly hawkish: the RBA has already delivered 75bp in rate hikes this year, inflation forecasts are elevated and rising, and Bullock explicitly warned of second-round effects and the risk of embedded inflation, while pledging to do 'what it considers necessary' to achieve price stability. No easing bias is expressed.
  • “Inflation is too high, and the Board will do what it considers necessary to achieve our mandate to deliver price stability and full employment.”
  • “If high inflation persists, it risks becoming embedded in price and wage-setting behaviour...That would result in more persistent inflation and would require even higher interest rates, and for longer, to return inflation to target.”
